Post-Extraction Urban Futures

Coal City Renewal – Fuxin after extraction

What happens after coal? This project re-frames industrial scarlands as reservoirs, timber fields, wetland parks, and civic gathering grounds, turning extraction residue into the groundwork for long-term livelihoods.

Location: Fuxin, China Type: Regenerative planning + urban landscape Year: 2023
← Back to projects

Project overview

Fuxin faces a post-industrial question shared by coal cities worldwide: what lives remain possible after energy extraction ceases? Instead of erasing the mine, this design outlines a layered transformation: pits become reservoirs, spoil heaps become timber terraces, conveyor belts become elevated paths, and central voids become civic commons.

Design strategy

  • Mines → Reservoirs – Stabilize pits, introduce wetlands, buffer seasonal runoff, and store long-term water security.
  • Spoil → Timber – Reforest spoil mounds with production forestry and carbon-sink planting, creating a new slow economy.
  • Industrial traces → Civic platforms – Preserve conveyors, gantries, and rail beds as elevated walks and viewing decks that keep the memory of extraction visible.
