Coastal Landscape Regeneration

Thermal Lagoons – Turning waste heat into renewal

A coastal energy landscape reimagined as a distributed ecosystem: industrial discharge becomes a resource for coral nurseries, aquaculture, public bathing, and soft infrastructures that stitch ecology back into everyday culture.

Location: Coastal Taiwan Type: Regenerative landscape proposal Year: 2024
← Back to projects

Project overview

Thermal discharge from energy facilities creates ecological imbalance, yet it also holds latent capacity for new life. Instead of treating waste heat as pollution alone, the project frames it as a catalyst for species recovery, aquaculture, and social ritual. Lagoons become biological incubators and civic infrastructure at once.

Design strategy

  • Waste → Energy – Redirect thermal flow to warm coral nurseries and accelerate early-stage growth.
  • Infrastructure → Habitat – Convert discharge channels into tidal pools, oyster beds, and mangrove edges.
  • Industry → Culture – Public bathing platforms create new social rituals around repaired ecology.

My role

Mapping energy gradients, programming lagoon zoning, producing 3D sectional sequences, aquaculture scenario modeling, public space visualization, offshore habitat simulation planning.

Project info

  • Focus – Blue energy ecologies
  • Method – Phasing, hydro-morphology studies
  • Scale – Regional + community coastal interface
